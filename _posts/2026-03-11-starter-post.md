Welcome to my blog(?), which will also be used for the purpose of posting and documenting some random things that I (will, hopefully) build! This is a starter post to get things going.

I don't expect any of these posts to be read (or tools, used), but I suppose there is an implied sense of accountability in posting something to the public, as compared to, say, a personal journal entry, or a hastily written markdown note. While the latter forms of "sloppiness" are certainly fine, it has occurred to me that I now seldom have the opportunity to write "seriously" (as compared to when I was an undergrad), and it might be admirable to try and retain whatever written voice and rhetorical capability I have presently, as hopeless as that may be, given my chosen profession.

I am also starting this technical blog so that I can attempt to use (learn the use of) "agentic coding" to create value in applications that I find useful (no matter how minute that value is). It seems that every week, there is some new development or progress in AI-assisted coding, and from what I know (at least from what very trusted, respectable peers tell me), these things are already quite amazing, and so I suppose that behind the inception of this blog is also some deeply (or not so deeply) embedded desire to not fall too far behind in this new "medium." All of this was meant to serve doubly as a disclaimer that, well, most coding in any future technical implement posted here is likely to be majority AI-generated code. This is a disclaimer because just a few months ago, an application being vibe coded was nearly a derogatory death knell of shame, whereas today it seems to be somewhat an accomplishment(?), and I'm really not sure _what_ exactly people will think of it some time into the future.

In any case, there are quite a number of additional reasons why I think starting something like this would be a good idea, most of which are probably self-evident, and I will leave for a future blog(?) post, which will likely be written when I feel inclined to pompously over-indulge in my self-importance and (alleged, implied) wisdom. I am only partially kidding here, and with regards to the not-kidding part, I can at least take solace in knowing that my writing style is so belabored to the point of being incomprehensible, and imbued with failed attempts at humor that humorously manage to always miss the mark, that no large language model fine-tuned by a company that hopes to stay solvent would even attempt to imitate, for doing so would certainly and doubly fail every benchmark that lies non-orthogonally to any conceivable, modern notion of "utility." That said, when there is an intent to be expressed, I hope the expression is as clean as can be.
